Frequently asked questions

A few things worth knowing.

Anything else? Contact support.

What is RiderLens?

RiderLens is a mobile app for mountain bikers that analyzes short riding clips. Pick a filmed jump, select the moment, and RiderLens draws your body position on every frame so you can study takeoff, air, and landing in detail.

Who is RiderLens for?

Gravity riders — dirt jump, enduro, downhill, bike park — who want to understand what actually happened on a jump and watch their technique progress attempt by attempt.

What footage works best?

A short clip filmed from the side with the whole rider in frame. Film with your phone's normal camera app (60fps captures the most detail), keep the clip under 30 seconds, then pick it in RiderLens and select up to 6 seconds around the action.

Is my footage private?

Your library lives on your phone — there are no accounts and no cloud library. Clips are uploaded only to be processed and are deleted from the server shortly after. Crash reports contain technical details only, never your videos. See the privacy policy.

Is RiderLens free?

Yes — you can analyze 3 clips every month for free, and playback, your library, and sharing are never limited. RiderLens Pro unlocks unlimited analyses with monthly or yearly billing.

Is RiderLens available for iPhone and Android?

RiderLens is launching on the App Store and Google Play. Analysis requires an internet connection; your records play back offline.

Can I cancel RiderLens Pro?

Yes. Subscriptions are billed and managed by the Apple App Store or Google Play — cancel anytime from your store account settings, and Pro stays active until the end of the paid period.
